## Design Context

### Users

Polyglot indie hackers and small-team developers who ship across many repos. They
arrive from a link on HN, a dev newsletter, or a GitHub README. They want a fast
answer to "what is gitjoin?" and a low-friction path to the repo. They respect
craft and are allergic to marketing-speak.

### Brand Personality

Warm, opinionated, quietly confident. Feels like a tool recommended by a friend
who has strong taste. Three words: **craft, precision, restraint**. The page
should evoke a late-night text editor — focused, dim, intentional — not a
product launch.

### Aesthetic Direction

**One-link landing page, unusual by design.** The single CTA sits in the middle
of the page, visually surrounded by a lattice of small "repo" blocks that
compose into one unified whole — the metaphor for a virtual monorepo rendered
directly in the layout. The page argues its thesis through its own composition.

- **Theme**: dark, warm charcoal. Text-editor-at-night, not gamer RGB.
- **No** gradients on text. **No** glow. **No** glassmorphism. **No** neon.
- **No** feature grid, testimonial rows, pricing, hero-with-blob, or any other
  SaaS template shape.
- Typography pairs a warm variable sans with a functional mono used for literal
  labels (not decorative "tech vibes").
- Motion is used once, intentionally, on page load: scattered blocks converge
  into the lattice. Respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.

### Anti-references

Explicitly NOT: generic SaaS homepages, Vercel/Linear clones (dark + cyan/purple
gradients + Inter), corporate consulting pages, cartoonish dev-tool mascot sites.

### Design Principles

1. **The layout is the argument.** Many blocks forming one whole = virtual
   monorepo. Don't explain the metaphor in copy; let the composition carry it.
2. **One link, one purpose.** Every pixel serves the single CTA. No nav, no
   secondary actions, no footer chrome beyond a quiet copyright.
3. **Restraint over flourish.** Dim palette, tight tracking, 1px borders, 2px
   gaps. No decorative effects that don't reinforce the metaphor.
4. **Craft the block, not the page.** The repo cells are the primary artifact —
   aligned, varied in subtle tints, readable. The rest is supporting chrome.
5. **Dark-mode done right.** Warm neutrals, not black. Off-white text, not pure
   white. Accent used at most twice on the page.
